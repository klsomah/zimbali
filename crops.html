What We Grow

Our Crops

Rare leaves, vibrant vegetables, fresh herbs, and some of the world's hottest peppers — all grown with care.

🍃
Category 01

Exotic Leaves

Culturally rich leafy greens prized in African, Caribbean, and Latin American cuisines.

🌿

Jute Leaf

A tender, slightly mucilaginous green popular in West African and Middle Eastern cooking — known as ewedu or mulukhiyah.

🍠

Sweet Potato Leaves

Nutritious and mild, these leaves are used fresh or cooked in soups and stews across Africa and Asia.

🌱

Fever Leaf

A medicinal and culinary herb traditionally used to treat fevers and inflammation. Distinctive aromatic flavor.

🌿

Cassava Leaf (Yuca)

A staple in Central and West African kitchens — slow-cooked to release rich, earthy flavor in stews and sauces.

🍃

Leaf of Life

Also known as Miracle Leaf or Kalanchoe. Celebrated in Caribbean folk medicine and as a culinary ingredient.

🌾

Malabar Spinach

A tropical climbing plant with thick, glossy leaves — a heat-tolerant alternative to regular spinach with a mild taste.

🌿
Category 02

Greens & Herbs

Fragrant, fresh, and essential — the backbone of countless recipes around the world.

🥬

Collard Greens

A Southern staple and African diaspora classic — hearty leaves with a robust flavor, best slow-braised or sautéed.

🥦

Cabbage

Versatile and crisp, used raw in slaws or cooked in everything from jollof to stir-fry.

🌿

Basil

Sweet and aromatic — a globally beloved herb essential in Italian, Thai, and many African-Caribbean cuisines.

🌱

Cilantro

Bright and citrusy, cilantro is a must-have garnish and flavor base in Latin American, African, and Asian cooking.

🥬
Category 03

Fresh Vegetables

Garden favorites and exotic varieties harvested at peak ripeness for maximum flavor.

🍠

Sweet Potato

Naturally sweet and nutrient-dense — a versatile root vegetable beloved worldwide in both savory and sweet dishes.

🧅

Onions

The foundation of flavor in virtually every cuisine — crisp, pungent, and indispensable in the kitchen.

🍅

Tomato

Juicy and vibrant — a garden essential that shines in sauces, salads, stews, and everything in between.

🥒

Cucumber

Cool, crisp, and refreshing — perfect fresh, pickled, or blended into drinks and salads.

🌽

Corn

Sweet and satisfying — grilled, boiled, or ground, corn is a crop that feeds culture across every continent.

🫑

Bell Peppers

Colorful and sweet — rich in vitamins and a staple ingredient in stir-fries, stews, and fresh preparations.

🍆

Eggplant

Silky and hearty when cooked — central to dishes like baba ganoush, ratatouille, and Nigerian garden egg stew.

🫚

Bitter Ball

Also called garden egg or African eggplant — a small, bitter variety prized in West African sauces and soups.

🌿

Kittely

A traditional tropical crop with a distinctively earthy flavor, popular in West African and Caribbean culinary traditions.

🎃

Pumpkin

Deeply golden and full of nutrition — the flesh, seeds, and leaves all find use in traditional cooking.

🌶️
Category 04

Fiery Peppers

From beloved heat to world-record fire — not for the faint of heart.

🌶️

Scotch Bonnet

🔥🔥🔥 Hot

The soul of Caribbean cooking — fruity, fiery, and utterly aromatic. The backbone of jerk sauces and pepper sauces.

~100,000–350,000 SHU
🌶️

Carolina Reaper

🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥 Extreme

Formerly the world's hottest pepper — twisted, gnarly, and devastatingly hot with surprising fruity undertones.

~1.5–2.2 million SHU
🌶️

Ghost Pepper (Bhut Jolokia)

🔥🔥🔥🔥 Extreme

Once the world's hottest — smoky, slow-building heat that sneaks up on you. A legend in spice culture worldwide.

~1 million SHU
🌶️

Apocalypse Scorpion

🔥🔥🔥🔥🔥 Nuclear

One of the most extreme peppers on the planet — scorpion-tailed, intensely hot, and reserved for the truly bold.

~1.5–2+ million SHU
⚠️ Our hot peppers are grown for culinary, hot sauce, and specialty use. Handle with care. Always use gloves when preparing.
